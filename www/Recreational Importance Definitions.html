Recreational Importance Definitions

 

Rank: Denotes the rank of a fish species based on this factor.

 

Factor Score: Denotes the scaled initial factor score for each species. Calculated based on the initial factor score standardized to the maximum initial factor score and scaled to range between 10, the maximum score, and 0, the minimum score.

 

Initial Factor Score: Unscaled factor score based on the coastwide pseudo revenue by species exponentiated by a value of 0.18 to account for highly variable coastwide pseudo revenues by species.

 

Pseudo Revenue Coastwide: The summed pseudo revenue across states based on the retained recreational catch and the pre-specified relative importance weight for each species by (retained catch by state and species multiplied by the relative importance weight by state and species).

 

Pseudo Revenue CA: The retained recreational catch multiplied by the state-specific relative importance for California.

 

Pseudo Revenue OR: The retained recreational catch multiplied by the state-specific relative importance for Oregon.

 

Pseudo Revenue WA: The retained recreational catch multiplied by the state-specific relative importance for Washington.

 

Relative Weight CA: The pre-specified relative importance for a recreational species in California.

 

Relative Weight OR: The pre-specified relative importance for a recreational species in Oregon.

 

Relative Weight WA: The pre-specified relative importance for a recreational species in Washington.

 

Retained Catch Coastwide: The sum of the retained recreational catch by state and species.

 

Retained Catch CA: The sum of the retained recreational catch in California by species.

 

Retained Catch OR: The sum of the retained recreational catch in Oregon by species.

 

Retained Catch WA: The sum of the retained recreational catch in Washington by species.

 

Management Group: Management group associated with a species within the fishery management plan.
