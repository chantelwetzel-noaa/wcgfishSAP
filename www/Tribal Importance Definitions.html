Tribal Importance Definitions

 

Rank: Denotes the rank of a fish species based on this factor.

 

Factor Score: The sum of the subsistence score plus the initial factor score. Values range between 10, the maximum score, and 0, the minimum score.

 

Subsistence Score: A pre-specified relative importance weight by species to the tribal fishery. Values range between 3.0, maximum score, and 0, the minimum score.

 

Initial Factor Score: Denotes the scaled initial factor score for each species. Calculated based on the initial factor score standardized to the maximum initial factor score and scaled to range between 7, the maximum score, and 0, the minimum score.

 

Interim Value: Unscaled factor score based on the tribal revenue by species exponentiated by a value of 0.18 to account for highly variable revenues by species.

 

Revenue: Total revenue based on the inflation adjusted ex-vessel revenue by species caught within tribal fisheries. Source: PacFIN.

 

Management Group: Management group associated with a species within the fishery management plan.
